  <div class="about-body">

  <p>Civil Engineering → Atmospheric & Oceanic Sciences (IISc) → Quant Finance → Reinforcement Learning → GenAI. On paper it looks like indecision. It isn't.</p>

  <p>I took up civil engineering out of love for mechanics, which grew into a fascination for fluid dynamics at IISc. That passion for applied math only deepened. Back in 2014–2015, statistical learning was finding widespread application everywhere — I wanted to test these ideas in industry. Quant finance gave me that playground, and that's where I fell in love with stochastic control and RL.</p>

  <p>Since then, whether working with deep generative models, alignment challenges, or enterprise-scale GenAI in the energy sector — the throughline has always been the same: using math and physics to solve real problems. Someday I know I'll return to teaching and studying physics, because the learning never really stops.</p>

  <p>This journal is a natural extension of that. I completed the Vizuara LLM from scratch course — 30 lectures building GPT-2 from the ground up. Most people treat that as a destination. I treated it as the starting line. The experiments here are about going deeper: understanding what the training curves are actually saying, what breaks stability, what doesn't, and why. The research thesis is stability analysis of small language models across pretraining, SFT, and RLVR fine-tuning.</p>

  <p>If you're on a similar path — learning by doing, not just by reading — this is for you.</p>
